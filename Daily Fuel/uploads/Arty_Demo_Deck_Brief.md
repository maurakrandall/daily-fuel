# Arty Creative Brief — Demo Day Deck
### Daily Fuel · SnapCamp Cohort 7 · May 21, 2026

---

## CONTEXT

Maura has a 5-minute frontend demo tomorrow. She needs a 7-slide deck that matches the Golden Hour v2 design system you already built. The deck supports a ~75 second slide run, then she switches to a live demo on Ember. It is NOT a pitch deck — it's a fast, confident product intro for peers in her bootcamp cohort.

**Your Golden Hour v2 deck is the design reference.** Same palette, same typography, same sun metaphor, same generous spacing. This deck should feel like it was born from the same system.

---

## LOCKED RAILS — do not change

**Color palette:**
- Warm cream background: #FDFBF7
- Deep navy text: #0A192F
- Gold accents: #D4AF37

**Typography:**
- Fraunces (italic serif) for accent/emphasis words
- Clean sans-serif for body text
- Generous spacing throughout

**Sun metaphor:** Use the sun graphic from Golden Hour v2 where appropriate (title slide, visual direction slide). It's the visual identity of the product.

**Format:** 7 slides. 16:9. No transitions or animations needed — this will be presented from a screen share.

## THE 7 SLIDES — exact content and direction

### SLIDE 1 — Title
**Content:**
- "Daily Fuel" (large, Fraunces italic for "Fuel")
- Tagline: "Close the gap. Not with tracking. With support."
- Sun graphic from Golden Hour v2
- Small: "dailyfuel.io" and "Maura Randall"

**Direction:** This is the cover of the Golden Hour deck energy — warm, confident, minimal. The sun is the hero.

---

### SLIDE 2 — Why It Matters
**Content:**
- Large stat callouts (the kind you do well — big numbers, small labels):
  - "30M" — Americans on GLP-1 medications
  - "88%" — below recommended protein intake
  - "0%" — met fiber recommendations
- One line below the stats: "The tools they've always used were built for a different body."

**Direction:** Let the numbers breathe. No clutter. Deep navy numbers with gold accent on the "0%." These stats are the emotional hook — they need to land visually.

---

### SLIDE 3 — What It Is / What It's Not
**Content — two columns or two distinct zones:**

**IS:**
- A gap-closer, not a tracker
- State-based — asks how eating feels today, adapts the plan
- Celebration-first — users see what they're gaining, never what they're missing

**IS NOT:**
- A calorie counter
- A diet app
- Streaks, scores, red numbers, or deficit language

**Direction:** "IS" side should feel warm and affirming (gold or cream tones). "IS NOT" side should feel struck-through or deliberately cooled — these are the things we're rejecting. You could use a subtle ✕ or strikethrough treatment on the "not" items.

---

### SLIDE 4 — Visual Direction: Golden Hour
**Content:**
- Slide title: "Golden Hour" (Fraunces italic)
- 2-3 key design principles as short text:
  - "Sun rises as your totals grow"
  - "Horizon, not progress bar"
  - "Celebration without confetti"
- 3-4 phone mockups from your Golden Hour v2 deck (pick the most compelling: state selector, plan with selections, celebration, saved plan)

**Direction:** This is your showcase. Pull from what you already built. The mockups do the talking — the principles are captions, not the hero. This slide should make people say "oh, that's beautiful."

---

### SLIDE 5 — V1: In and Out
**Content — two zones:**

**IN (with screen previews):**
- Onboarding that builds your food profile (show 1-2 onboarding screens)
- State selector → daily plan (show state selector mockup)
- Celebratory selection model (show plan with tapped cards)
- Close the Gap
- Shot-cycle-aware notifications

**OUT (with ✕ marks):**
- ✕ Calorie data (never)
- ✕ Macro tracking (earned later)
- ✕ Community features (long game)

**Direction:** "In" side should feel alive — the screen previews from your existing mockups make it tangible. "Out" side should be visually lighter/smaller — a short list with clear ✕ marks. The contrast between "look at what's here" and "this is what we're deliberately not building yet" should be immediate.

---

### SLIDE 6 — Live Demo
**Content:**
- Only the words: "Live Demo"
- Nothing else. Centered. Navy text on cream.

**Direction:** Maximum white space. This is a transition moment — Maura switches to Ember screen share here. The slide should be the visual equivalent of a deep breath before the show.

---

### SLIDE 7 — Questions & Feedback
**Content:**
- "Questions?" or "What questions do you have?" (Fraunces italic)
- Small sun graphic (settled state, not bloomed — like the completed plan sun)
- "Daily Fuel · dailyfuel.io · Maura Randall"

**Direction:** Calm, settled, warm. This stays on screen during 2 minutes of peer feedback. It should feel like the end of a golden hour — the day settling.

---

## WHAT TO AVOID

- Don't make it look like a pitch deck for investors — this is a peer demo, not a fundraise
- No bullet-heavy slides — let the screen mockups and stats do the work
- No progress bars, trackers, or clinical imagery anywhere
- Don't crowd the slides — at 10-15 seconds per slide, less is more
- Don't use any new screens or layouts that aren't in the existing Golden Hour v2 system
