Appendix
Design notes
Shared principles across all three directions, animation ideas for the next round, and the questions a usability test should answer.
05 / 05

Shared principles

  • Language. "Intention," "in your corner," "boundaries," "leave out" — never "tracking," "goals," "restrictions."
  • One question per screen. Goals became a 3-preset choice (gentle / steady / stronger); numbers are secondary.
  • "Closing the gap" seeded on the celebration — tomorrow's progress ring introduces the concept without teaching it yet.
  • Skip is first-class everywhere. Every screen has a soft out.
  • Disclaimer present, never dominant. Small, italic, below the fold.

Transitions (next round)

  • Content fades up 12px; background color/shape parallaxes at ~40% speed — premium, meditative.
  • Celebration hero (sun / glow / ritual sun) scales 40% → 100% on entry, 480ms ease-out.
  • Pill selection micro-tap: 120ms scale-down to 0.96, haptic-equivalent feedback.
  • Screen-to-screen horizontal slide at 360ms, cubic-bezier(.2, .8, .2, 1).

Usability test priorities

  • Does "intention / in your corner / boundaries" feel warm or cringe? Ritual vs. Soft Current will tell us.
  • Do presets replace the numeric input, or do users want to see numbers? A skippable "set exact numbers" link tests demand.
  • Does the tomorrow preview on celebration feel like a payoff — or a premature obligation?
  • Which direction most reads as "calm, not clinical"?
